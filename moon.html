Things to be brought

  • Space Suit: A specially designed space suit is the most important piece of clothing to wear in space.

  • Special Footwear: Space boots or slipper-like shoes designed for microgravity environments are essential to prevent floating and to protect your feet.

  • Personal Items: You can bring personal items for comfort and entertainment, such as family photos, books, music, or small mementos. However, space is limited, so personal items must be carefully chosen.

  • First Aid kit: If you have any prescribed medications or specific medical needs, you should bring them along and inform the medical team.

  • Snacks and Meals: Astronauts have carefully planned meals and snacks, often in the form of dehydrated or freeze-dried foods. These are chosen for their nutritional value and ease of preparation in space.

  • Documentation: You should carry important documentation, including your Space passport and any necessary travel or medical documents.

  • Mission-Specific Equipment: Depending on the mission and your role, you may need specialized tools or equipment. Astronauts often carry out experiments, repairs, and maintenance tasks in space.
